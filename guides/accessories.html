Accessories & Magical Power

Accessory Bag, Magical Power system, upgrade tiers, Maxwell's Powers, and key accessories at every stage.

v0.21

Accessories are items stored in your Accessory Bag that provide passive bonuses and contribute to your Magical Power (MP). MP determines how strong your Power Stone bonuses are and how many Tuning Points you get from Maxwell. Building a strong accessory collection is one of the most impactful long-term investments in SkyBlock.

Accessory Bag

The Accessory Bag unlocks at Redstone Collection II. You need Redstone Minion T1 to reach this — get it early. The bag holds all your accessories without taking up inventory space. Accessories only contribute MP and their effects when stored here (or held in inventory) — not in chests, Ender Chests, or backpacks.

The bag expands as you reach higher Redstone collection milestones, giving more slots over time.

Magical Power

Each accessory in your bag adds MP based on its rarity:

  • Common: 3 MP
  • Uncommon: 5 MP
  • Rare: 8 MP
  • Epic: 12 MP
  • Legendary: 16 MP
  • Mythic: 22 MP

Only the highest tier of each accessory line counts. If you have Speed Talisman and Speed Ring, only the Ring contributes. Recombobulating an accessory (with Recombobulator 3000) increases its rarity by one and therefore its MP contribution.

Hegemony Artifact is special — it counts double its own MP contribution. At Mythic rarity that's +44 MP from one item. It's mandatory for anyone pushing high MP.

Upgrading Accessories

Most accessories come in a line with 2–4 tiers:

  • Talisman (Common/Uncommon) → Ring (Rare) → Artifact (Epic) → Relic (Legendary)
  • Some lines are longer, like the Crux line from the Rift which has 7 tiers up to Celestial Starstone
  • Higher tier = more MP + usually better stats/effects

Beyond upgrading tiers, you can Recombobulate accessories for +1 rarity and add Enrichments to Legendary+ accessories for minor stat boosts. Enrichments can be swapped across all accessories with an Enrichment Swapper (5,000 Bits at Elizabeth).

Maxwell & the Power System

Maxwell NPC (found in the Wizard Tower) is where you select a Power and spend Tuning Points. This system replaced the old per-accessory reforging in a major update.

  • Power: Pick one active Power — each gives different stat scaling based on your total MP. For example, Sighted gives Crit Damage, Bizarre gives Strength, Silky gives all stats. Pick what fits your build.
  • Tuning Points: You get 1 Tuning Point per 10 MP. Spend them at Maxwell to fine-tune individual stats. This is how endgame players min-max specific numbers.

MP Milestones to Target

  • 100–200 MP: Early game baseline — enough to feel the Power bonuses.
  • 400 MP: Midgame target. Tuning Points start to matter here.
  • 700–800 MP: Late game. Powers scale significantly at this range.
  • 1,000 MP: Major milestone — all powers become notably stronger, more Tuning flexibility.
  • 2,050 MP: Endgame maximum (base). Requires every accessory in the game.
  • 2,290 MP: Maximum including Dungeon Accessories, only counts inside Dungeons.
Do not store accessories in chests or backpacks — they only contribute MP and effects when in your Accessory Bag or held in inventory.
