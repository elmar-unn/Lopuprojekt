Carpentry Guide

Fast leveling methods, best furniture recipes for XP, profile customization, and the full progression from Carpentry 1 to 50.

v0.21

Introduction

Carpentry is one of the least gameplay-critical skills in SkyBlock, but dismissing it entirely is a mistake. It contributes meaningfully to your SkyBlock Level — the overall progression metric that gates certain content and cosmetics — and it unlocks the Profile Customization system, which lets you place and arrange furniture decorations across your private island. Every 10 Carpentry levels also provides a minor stat bonus (Health primarily).

The honest framing of Carpentry: it's a long-term passive goal, not an urgent priority. New players should not be sinking coins into rushing Carpentry while combat and farming skills lag behind. Mid- to late-game players with disposable coin income can start making consistent progress without feeling the cost. Getting to Carpentry 50 is a meaningful investment spread over weeks or months, not a weekend project.

Tip: If you want Carpentry XP without dedicating a separate session to it, craft furniture during downtime — while waiting for Bazaar orders to fill, between Dungeon runs, or while AFK on your island. The XP accumulates without requiring focused grinding.

How Carpentry XP Works

Every point of Carpentry XP comes from crafting furniture items at a Workbench. There is no other XP source — no Carpentry-related drops, no passive accumulation, no alternative method. You craft furniture, you get XP. The amount of XP per craft is fixed per recipe and visible in the Workbench UI before you commit to crafting.

The critical insight is that XP scales with the material cost and complexity of the recipe, not its visual appeal or rarity category. A simple item that costs cheap wood and gives 40 XP is often a better investment than a flashy higher-tier item that costs expensive materials and gives 150 XP but uses materials 10× more expensive. Always compare XP per coin spent, not XP per craft.

There's no grinding location for Carpentry. It's purely transactional: acquire materials from Bazaar, craft, repeat. Materials can be bought cheaply in bulk via buy orders, and the crafted furniture can either be placed on your island or simply discarded — the XP is awarded at the moment of crafting regardless of what you do with the item.

Profile Customization

The primary non-stat reward from Carpentry is access to the Profile Customization system. This lets you furnish and decorate your private island beyond the standard block-placing mechanics. As Carpentry levels up, new furniture types and decoration categories unlock progressively.

Items available through this system include:

  • Furniture (beds, chairs, tables, bookshelves, wardrobes)
  • Decorative props and lighting
  • Custom item display stands
  • Seasonal and limited furniture from Carpentry progression milestones

For players who invest heavily in island building and aesthetics, Carpentry 50 is meaningful. The full furniture catalog only opens at higher levels, and some of the more interesting decorative pieces require Carpentry 40+ to unlock. The island display system also interacts with player trophy items and gear displays, making it relevant for players who like showcasing their progression to visitors.

Best XP Methods

The approach shifts slightly across the level range, mainly because higher levels have tighter XP-per-coin ratios and require more expensive materials to stay efficient.

Early Method — Carpentry 1 to 30

At low levels the XP requirements per level are modest and cheap recipes are plentiful. The best approach is to identify the cheapest furniture recipes in the Workbench that still give at least 15–30 XP per craft, then buy materials in bulk via Bazaar buy orders and batch craft as many as possible in a single session.

Oak Logs, Spruce Logs, and their planks are the primary materials for low-level furniture. These are among the cheapest items on the Bazaar and can be bought in thousands for minimal cost. Small decorative items — basic stools, small shelves, simple frames — often hit the best XP/cost ratio in this range. Always check the Workbench XP display for each recipe before committing. Some early recipes that look cheap actually give surprisingly low XP.

Practical workflow: set a buy order for 2,000–5,000 logs, wait for it to fill (usually a few hours), craft in one sitting, repeat. The entire early stretch can be done in a handful of sessions over a week without meaningful cost impact.

Mid-Level Method — Carpentry 30 to 45

The XP required per level scales up significantly in this range. Basic wood recipes become less efficient in absolute XP-per-hour terms because the raw XP numbers are low compared to what mid-tier furniture gives. Recipes that incorporate Wool, Stone variants, or early enchanted materials become the new benchmark.

The Workbench shows the XP for every recipe, so the analytical approach is the same: divide the current Bazaar buy-order cost of the materials by the XP given, find the lowest cost-per-XP in the furniture list at your current level, and buy accordingly. This mid-range tends to cost a few hundred thousand to a million coins total spread across multiple sessions.

Tip: Recipes that use multiple material types (wood + wool + stone) often give more XP per craft than single-material recipes, even when the total material cost is similar. The game rewards recipe complexity in its XP formula. Check mixed-material furniture first when scouting for efficient recipes.

Endgame Method — Carpentry 45 to 50

The final stretch is the most expensive portion of Carpentry by a large margin. High-tier furniture requires materials like Enchanted logs, colored Enchanted Wool, and occasionally rare block types. The XP per craft is high in absolute terms but the material cost makes the cost-per-XP ratio worse than earlier tiers.

Most players who reach this range are doing so with accumulated wealth and treat it as a long-term goal rather than a sprint. The practical approach is to set buy orders for the top few recipes' materials, run a craft session whenever the orders fill, and not try to rush. Attempting to force Carpentry 45–50 quickly requires spending heavily into instant-buy prices, which makes an already expensive stretch dramatically worse.

Warning: Do not use instant-buy for Carpentry materials in the 45–50 range. The material volumes required and the premium over buy-order prices combine to make instant-buying here extremely costly. Set orders and wait.

Materials Needed

Almost all Carpentry recipes draw from the same pool of base materials. None of these require farming or special drops — everything is available on the Bazaar.

Common Materials (Early–Mid)

  • Oak / Spruce / Birch / Jungle Logs and Planks: The backbone of early furniture. Cheap in bulk. Buy whichever wood type the specific recipe calls for — they're interchangeable in cost.
  • Wool (all colors): Used extensively in mid-tier furniture for upholstery, cushions, and textiles. White Wool is the cheapest and most common. Colored variants cost slightly more.
  • Stone / Cobblestone / Stone Bricks: Common structural material for furniture like shelves, frames, and bases. Very cheap.
  • Glass / Glass Panes: Used in display cases, cabinets, and windows. Cheap.
  • Leather: Some mid-tier furniture uses leather for decorative purposes.

Advanced Materials (Late–Endgame)

  • Enchanted Oak Log / Enchanted Spruce Log: Required for high-tier wood furniture. Crafted from 160 logs or bought from Bazaar. Significantly more expensive than base logs.
  • Enchanted Wool: High-tier textile furniture. Cost scales with the color.
  • Enchanted Leather: Some endgame furniture recipes. More expensive than the base material.
  • Quartz variants: Some decorative high-tier furniture uses Quartz blocks and pillars.

Carpentry Stat Bonuses

Each Carpentry level provides a minor stat bonus, primarily Health. The bonuses are not large enough to drive prioritization decisions on their own — they're a nice side effect of leveling a skill you're working toward anyway. The full 50-level progression gives a total of roughly +100 Health spread across the levels, with specific bonus points at milestone levels.

Carpentry also contributes to SkyBlock Level at a rate comparable to other skills. For players specifically working toward a SkyBlock Level threshold (for profile rank, certain cosmetic unlocks, or personal goals), Carpentry levels are real contributors and worth counting in your progression plan.

Common Mistakes

  • Rushing Carpentry as a new player: The coin cost is real and those coins are better spent on combat gear, minions, or farming upgrades until you have a stable income. There's no content that requires high Carpentry urgently.
  • Crafting expensive furniture for the visual appeal rather than XP efficiency: A beautiful high-tier item that costs 500k in materials but gives only 200 XP is dramatically worse than several cheap items that give 50 XP each for 10k total. The XP display in the Workbench is the only metric that matters for leveling.
  • Not checking Workbench XP before crafting: Recipe XP amounts are not intuitive from item appearance. Some visually simple items give surprisingly good XP; some elaborate items give poor XP. Always read the number in the Workbench UI.
  • Instant-buying materials for bulk crafting runs: Especially painful at higher levels where material volumes are large. Always use buy orders for Carpentry material acquisition.
  • Neglecting Carpentry entirely through late game: It doesn't need to be a priority, but completely ignoring it means SkyBlock Level accumulates more slowly. A few crafting sessions per week during downtime is enough to make gradual progress without any meaningful time investment.
  • Not tracking material price fluctuations: Wool and log prices on the Bazaar can spike significantly during certain events or patch cycles. If material prices are unusually high, wait a few days rather than paying inflated rates for a non-urgent skill.
