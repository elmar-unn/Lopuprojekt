Farming Guide

Complete progression from Farming 1 to hypermax — crops, Garden, contests, Farming Fortune, gear, and best money-making setups.

v0.21

Introduction & Why Farming Matters

Farming is one of the most lucrative and mechanically deep skills in Hypixel SkyBlock. Unlike combat or mining, farming's income ceiling scales almost entirely with how much Farming Fortune you can stack — and the game gives you dozens of overlapping systems to push that number higher. At low Fortune you're looking at a few hundred thousand coins per hour. At hypermax Fortune across a dedicated crop you can realistically clear 4–6 million coins per hour active, making it competitive with mid-tier dungeon runs and significantly more consistent.

Beyond raw income, Farming unlocks systems that compound on each other. Reaching Farming 10 opens Jacob's Farming Contests, the game's best recurring source of Jacob's Tickets. Those tickets buy Turbo-Crop enchant books, which are among the biggest single-item Farming Fortune upgrades in the game. Jacob's Contests also award Gold medals, and Gold medals per crop are the only way to raise the Farming skill cap above 50 — each Gold in a specific crop raises that crop's contribution to your cap by 1, up to a hard ceiling of Farming 60. That cap increase directly adds Farming XP capacity, which feeds back into more passive Farming Fortune from skill levels.

At Farming 50, you unlock the ability to wear Helianthus Armor, the endgame farming set. Helianthus combines the bonuses of the four preceding farming armor tiers (Melon, Cropie, Squash, Fermento) into a single suit with a massive additional Farming Fortune multiplier on top. Getting there requires months of consistent play — leveling plots in the Garden, stacking Gold medals, and grinding toward 100 million total crops broken for the Cultivating X enchant.

Farming XP also contributes directly to your overall SkyBlock level, which gates access to certain island upgrades, cosmetics, and progression milestones. Players who neglect farming often hit a SkyBlock level wall around 200–250 that forces them to catch up their gathering skills. Starting farming early and doing contests consistently — even with a mediocre setup — builds a foundation that pays off through every stage of the game.

This guide covers everything: how Farming Fortune works mathematically, every crop and how it scales, all gear and pet choices at each progression stage, the Garden system in full, contest strategy, and the coin-per-hour reality of each setup.

Farming Fortune — The Core Mechanic

Every farming decision you make should be evaluated through one lens: how much Farming Fortune does it give me for this crop? Farming Fortune (FF) is a stat that directly multiplies how many crops drop when you break a block. The formula is straightforward:

Total crops dropped = base drops × (1 + Farming Fortune / 100)

At 0 FF you get the base 1× drops. At 100 FF you get 2× drops. At 200 FF you get 3× drops. At 500 FF you get 6× drops. At 900 FF you get 10× drops. This scales linearly with no diminishing returns, which makes every point of Farming Fortune equally valuable regardless of how much you already have. That's why stacking FF is the correct priority at every stage of progression — there is no point at which adding more FF stops being worth it.

It's also worth understanding that Farming Fortune is partly crop-specific. Some sources apply universally (skill levels, pet abilities, plot bonuses), while others only apply to a specific crop (Turbo-Crop enchants). This means your effective Farming Fortune for, say, Sugar Cane will be higher than your Fortune for Melon if you've invested in Turbo-Cane but not Turbo-Melon. Tracking your per-crop FF is important when deciding which crops to farm for money versus contests.

Sources of Farming Fortune

Farming Fortune accumulates from many stacking sources. The major categories are below — the exact numbers here assume max investment in each source:

  • Farming skill passive: +1 Farming Fortune per skill level after level 1. At Farming 60 this is +59 FF universally.
  • Armor set bonuses: Each farming armor set gives large amounts of FF as a set bonus. Helianthus at full upgrade gives several hundred FF combined from all its stacked set effects. This is typically the single largest category.
  • Turbo-Crop enchants: Each crop has its own Turbo enchant on the hoe — Turbo-Wheat, Turbo-Cane, Turbo-Coco, Turbo-Pumpkin, etc. Each level of Turbo-[Crop] gives +5 Farming Fortune specifically for that crop, up to level 5 for +25 FF. These are bought with Jacob's Tickets from Anita, and levels 4–5 require having earned Bronze or Silver medals in that specific crop first.
  • Dedication enchant: Scales with your total collection count for a specific crop. The higher your lifetime collection, the more FF Dedication provides. At very high collection counts it can rival or exceed Turbo-Crop in FF value for that crop. Also bought from Anita.
  • Harvesting enchant: On the hoe itself — gives +10 Farming Fortune per level, up to Harvesting V for +50 FF universally.
  • Cultivating enchant: Unlocks at Farming 16. Levels up based on total crops broken on the hoe — starts at I and can reach X. At Cultivating X (unlocked after breaking 100 million crops with that hoe) it gives +200 FF. This is a long grind but one of the biggest single enchant bonuses in the game.
  • Pet abilities: The Legendary Elephant gives Farming Fortune passively. The exact value scales with the pet's level but can approach 100 FF at level 100.
  • Garden plot levels: Each Garden plot levels up 1–12 as you farm that crop on it. At max level, each plot grants significant permanent FF for the assigned crop. With all relevant plots maxed, this adds a substantial flat FF bonus.
  • Farmhand perk: An item you earn through Visitor trading milestones. Grants Farming Fortune based on how many unique Visitor trades you've completed. This scales up to 30+ FF at high visitor counts.
  • Pelts upgrades: Pelts are earned by killing Pests in the Garden. Spent at the Pest control NPC for permanent Farming Fortune upgrades. A fully upgraded pelt track adds meaningful FF.
  • Green Thumb: A collection reward from the Farming skill that gives a small flat FF bonus.
Key insight: Because FF scales linearly, the "best" source of Farming Fortune is simply whichever source costs the least effort for the most FF right now. Early game that's Melon Armor. Mid game that's Turbo-Crop books. Late game that's Cultivating X and Garden plot maxing. Always chase the next easiest gain.

Why Farming Fortune Outweighs Everything Else

Players sometimes ask whether they should spend coins on a different accessory or pet instead of farming fortune upgrades. The answer is almost always no. Because FF multiplies your entire crop output — and therefore your XP and coin income — every 100 additional FF is doubling your hourly yield relative to zero FF. Going from 400 FF to 500 FF raises your multiplier from 5× to 6×, a 20% increase in output for one upgrade. The dollar-per-percent efficiency of FF upgrades is almost always better than peripheral upgrades like walk speed accessories or minor stat boosters.

Sugar Cane — The Rancher's Crop

Sugar Cane is arguably the highest skill-ceiling crop in the game. The mechanic is simple: cane grows 2–4 blocks high, and breaking the bottom segment drops all segments above it simultaneously. This means you can set up long rows of cane and walk along breaking just the lowest segment of each column — one swing collects the entire column's worth of cane in a single drop event, and since Farming Fortune applies per break, a single hoe swing at the base of a 4-tall cane column with 900 FF yields roughly 40 cane from one block break.

The critical piece of Sugar Cane farming is walk speed. If you walk too fast you'll outpace the natural cane growth cycle and swing at empty farmland. If you walk too slow you waste time. Rancher's Boots solve this by letting you set a precise speed value. The community-optimal speed for a standard 2-wide cane row layout is around 400% walk speed, but this depends on your specific plot layout. Test and adjust using the speed dial on the Rancher's Boots.

With a Legendary Slug pet (gives bonus Sugar Cane drops on every break), Turbo-Cane V, Cultivating X, and full Helianthus gear, active Sugar Cane farming clears 3–4 million coins per hour at current Bazaar prices. Add a maxed-out cane plot in the Garden and you push toward the 4M ceiling. This makes Sugar Cane one of the two best farming income methods in the game.

Pumpkin — Highest Raw Ceiling

Pumpkin and Melon share almost identical mechanics — you break the pumpkin or melon block itself, not the stem, and the stem immediately re-queues to produce another block. The key difference from row crops is that pumpkins grow on dirt adjacent to the stem, so your farm layout needs enough stem-to-dirt ratio for maximum yield. A standard pumpkin farm has alternating rows of stems and harvest lanes.

Pumpkin consistently trades at higher Bazaar prices than most other crops due to demand from Minion fuel and various crafting recipes. At hypermax — Legendary Elephant pet, Turbo-Pumpkin V, Cultivating X, Bountiful Theoretical Hoe of Wheat/Pumpkin, all Garden plots maxed, full Helianthus — pumpkin farming yields 4–6 million coins per hour, occasionally higher during Bazaar price spikes. This is the highest reliable income ceiling of any single farming crop.

Unlike Sugar Cane, Pumpkin doesn't require precise speed management. You sprint through your harvest lane clicking continuously. The bottleneck is purely Farming Fortune and plot layout density. Pumpkin is also one of the easier crops to set up well early on, which makes it a good target for mid-game players who don't yet have Rancher's Boots.

Melon — The Stepping Stone

Melon is mechanically identical to Pumpkin but typically sells for slightly less. More importantly, Melon is the basis of the early-to-mid armor progression — crafting Enchanted Melon is a prerequisite for Melon Armor, and farming Melon while wearing Melon Armor causes Cropie/Squash plants to drop, unlocking the next armor tier. Even if you don't intend to farm Melon long-term for money, you will spend time farming it while progressing toward Fermento and Helianthus. At hypermax it sits around 3–4M coins/hr, slightly below Pumpkin.

Wheat, Carrot, Potato — The Easy Entry

These three crops all use the same hoe-on-farmland mechanic, they all auto-replant with the Replenish enchant, and they're the easiest farms to build on your private island before you unlock the Garden. Their income ceiling is significantly lower than Pumpkin or Cane even at max setup — roughly 1–1.5M coins/hr at hypermax — but they have one critical use: Visitor trades. Garden Visitors frequently request large quantities of Wheat, Carrot, or Potato in exchange for Jacob's Tickets, rare drops, or coins. Keeping several stacks of each in storage ensures you never miss a valuable trade.

Cactus & Nether Wart

Both crops have niche use cases. Cactus grows quickly and the auto-break mechanic means it can be farmed very efficiently with the right layout, but the Bazaar price per crop is lower. Nether Wart is in demand for brewing recipes and Visitor trades; it grows on Soul Sand and can be placed in reasonably dense plots. Neither crop approaches Pumpkin or Cane income at max, but they're worth having a plot or two assigned in the Garden for visitor trade coverage.

Jacob's Farming Contest — Deep Guide

Jacob's Farming Contest is the single most important recurring event in SkyBlock farming. It runs once every SkyBlock day — that's once every real-world hour — and lasts exactly 20 minutes. Jacob announces three random crops at the start of each SkyBlock day. If you can farm any of those three crops, you should be in the Garden doing so when the contest opens.

The ranking system is global and relative. Your medal depends solely on where you place compared to every other player farming that same crop across the entire server at that moment. There are no fixed thresholds based on absolute crop count — just percentiles. This means your placement is partly a function of how many competitive players are online and farming that crop. Some crops at off-peak hours are much easier to Gold or Diamond than others during prime time.

To participate at all, you need Farming 10. The absolute minimum to earn any reward is 100 crops collected during the contest window. You need to actually collect the crops in your inventory — pests killing crops or Composter consumption don't count. Always farm during the contest in a standard harvest loop with crops going into your inventory.

Gold Medals and the Farming Cap

Every Gold medal you earn in a specific crop raises that crop's contribution to your maximum Farming skill cap by +1. The base cap is Farming 50. Each of the 9 main crops can raise the cap by up to +1 (some sources indicate up to +10 total possible raises, bringing the hard cap to 60). In practice this means you need to earn a Gold medal in each crop you care about leveling through — if you only ever Gold in Sugar Cane, you can only raise the cap related to that crop's XP contribution. Getting at least one Gold in every crop type over time is the path to Farming 60.

Cap warning: Many players reach Farming 50 and then stop gaining Farming XP because they haven't unlocked any cap raises. You must earn Gold medals in contests to unlock cap levels 51 through 60. Do not wait until late game to start working on this — every contest you skip early is a Gold medal you could have earned.

Pest Preparation — The Contest Exploit

The single biggest edge you can get in a Jacob's Contest (outside of pure gear) is pre-spawning pests. Here's how it works: pests spawn naturally on Garden plots over time. You can stockpile up to 8 pests sitting alive on your Garden plots at once, plus an additional 12 trapped in Vermin Traps, for a total of 20 pests ready to kill. When you kill a pest, it drops a burst of crops from the assigned plot's crop type. Kill 20 pests at the exact moment a contest opens and you instantly bank a large crop count — potentially several thousand crops before you've even broken a single block yourself.

This tactic is most effective when the contest crop matches your best-prepared plot. If you maintain a maxed Sugar Cane plot with pests stockpiled and Sugar Cane comes up in a contest, killing all 20 pests at contest start can push you from a Bronze threshold straight to Platinum or Diamond before the 20-minute farming window even starts in earnest. Smart players check which crops Jacob will announce (tracked by fan-made tools and SkyBlock calendars) and pre-fill pests on the matching crop in advance.

Managing pests between contests is an ongoing task. Pests that aren't in Vermin Traps and aren't killed quickly will reduce plot efficiency over time. The workflow most farming-focused players use: let pests accumulate to near-max before a contest that matches your crop, kill them at contest start, then resume normal pest management afterward.

Contest Strategy

Know your crops. Before every contest, check which three crops Jacob announced and compare them to your per-crop Farming Fortune. If Sugar Cane is in the contest and your Turbo-Cane is maxed but your Turbo-Wheat is level 1, you should be on cane regardless of how easy the Wheat contest looks. Your Fortune advantage on a strong crop far outweighs the competition level on a weaker crop.

During the 20-minute window: sprint constantly, never stop breaking blocks, keep your inventory clear of junk so you don't fill up and miss drops, and kill any pests you pre-stockpiled at the very start. If you have a Bountiful-reforged hoe and Elephant pet equipped, your output is maximized. Some players switch to a Mooshroom Cow for Mushroom-specific contests — know your alternatives before the contest starts so you're not fumbling with inventory menus mid-run.

After consistently Gold- or Platinum-medaling in 2–3 specific crops, you'll accumulate Jacob's Tickets fast enough to buy Turbo-Crop books on a regular cadence. Target Diamond in your best 1–2 crops rather than Bronze in every crop — the ticket differential between Diamond (35) and Bronze (10) is more than 3× the reward for probably less than 2× the effort gap once your setup is strong.

Jacob's Tickets — Anita's Shop

Jacob's Tickets are the primary currency earned from contests. Anita's Shop (she stands near Jacob in the Hub) sells several key items:

  • Turbo-Crop enchant books (I–V): One book per crop type. Each level costs an increasing number of tickets and gives +5 Farming Fortune for that specific crop, stacking up to +25 FF at Turbo-[Crop] V. Levels 4 and 5 require you to have earned at least a Bronze or Silver medal in that crop as a prerequisite — you can't just buy them with tickets without having demonstrated competency in that crop. These are your most important purchases. Max all of them for your main 2–3 crops before anything else.
  • Dedication enchant books (I–IV): Collection-scaling Farming Fortune. The higher your lifetime crop collection for a specific crop, the more FF Dedication provides. Stacks with Turbo-Crop. Worth buying once you have high collection counts — the gain is front-loaded for players who've already farmed hundreds of millions of a single crop.
  • Music Rune: Used at the Harp instrument in the Hub, gives a musical note cosmetic effect. Not farming-relevant but some players want it for other questlines.

Prioritize your ticket spending by what gives the most Farming Fortune per ticket. Early on, Turbo-Crop I–III are very efficient per-ticket. Turbo-Crop IV and V cost significantly more but are still worth buying before any secondary purchases. Once your main crop's Turbo-Crop is maxed, move to Dedication for that crop, then repeat for your second crop.

The Garden

The Garden is a private farming island that unlocks once you've collected 100 different crop items across all crop types. This threshold is easily hit naturally — most players unlock the Garden within a few hours of starting to farm. Once unlocked, it becomes the permanent home of all serious farming activity. Your private island farms become largely irrelevant once you have the Garden set up.

The Garden provides 23 assignable crop plots. Each plot can be set to a specific crop type, and you physically build and maintain farms on those plots. The plots are spatially isolated, meaning each is a separate zone you can design independently. Most top farmers run 2–3 plots of their primary crop and single plots of secondary crops for Visitor trade coverage.

Plot Leveling

Each plot tracks a "Crop Milestone" counter for its assigned crop. As you farm that crop on that plot, the counter rises. At each of 12 milestone levels, the plot unlocks a permanent Farming Fortune bonus for that specific crop. Getting every plot assigned to your main crop to level 12 is a very long-term goal — it requires breaking tens of millions of that crop on each individual plot — but the total bonus is substantial. End-game farmers with all their cane or pumpkin plots at level 12 have 50+ FF just from plot milestones alone.

Plot leveling is passive: it happens automatically as you farm normally. You don't need to do anything special. The only active choice is making sure your plots are assigned to crops you actually farm frequently, since a plot assigned to a crop you never farm won't level.

Visitors

Visitors are NPC characters who spawn at the Garden entrance periodically and request crop bundles in exchange for various rewards. Each visitor has a rarity tier (Copper, Iron, Gold, Diamond) and the rewards scale accordingly. Copper tier visitors — which are the most common — typically offer small coin payouts or basic XP. Iron and Gold visitors may offer Jacob's Tickets, rare crafting materials, or mid-tier accessories. Diamond tier visitors (the rarest, such as Duchess, Odger, and Beth) can drop unique items that aren't obtainable any other way, including components used for endgame accessory crafting.

The Farmhand item — a Farming Fortune accessory — is obtained by completing a certain total number of Visitor trades. The more unique visitors you've traded with, the more FF Farmhand provides. Players who trade every visitor they encounter will see this number grow naturally over months of play. Skipping visitors loses you this progress permanently, so always trade even with Copper visitors when possible.

Keep reserves of every crop type in your Garden storage. Visitors can ask for any crop, and the most valuable visitors (Gold/Diamond tier) often request crops you might not be actively farming. Having even a moderate stockpile of Wheat, Potato, Carrot, Nether Wart, and Cocoa Beans ensures you never miss a valuable trade because you're out of stock.

The Composter

The Composter converts surplus crops and fuel items into Organic Matter, which is used for several Garden upgrades and also feeds into broader SkyBlock crafting recipes. The Composter runs passively without your attention once it's loaded. Throw in the crops you generate from farming (particularly low-value crops like excess Wheat or Carrot that you don't need for trades) and let it convert them overnight. Upgrade the Composter's speed and capacity over time using Organic Matter and coins — faster composting means more output per day with the same input.

Organic Matter also fuels certain Garden upgrade unlocks. Don't sell all your low-value crops on the Bazaar before checking whether you need Organic Matter for an upcoming upgrade. The coin difference is usually small and the upgrade value is real.

The Pest System

Pests spawn on Garden plots as a passive background process. They come in several types (Fly, Mosquito, Mite, Cricket, etc.) and each type is associated with specific crops. When pests are alive on a plot, they reduce the plot's farming efficiency — you get fewer crops per swing than you would from a pest-free plot. Kill pests regularly using the Vacuum item (found in the Garden shop). Vacuum charges are consumed when you kill pests, so keep it replenished.

Killing pests drops Pest materials used for crafting pest-specific accessories and cosmetics. Pests also drop Pelts, a currency spent at the Pest control vendor for permanent Farming Fortune upgrades. The pelt upgrade track gives meaningful FF bonuses at higher investment levels — consistent pest killing over weeks of play accumulates enough Pelts to meaningfully move your FF upward.

As discussed in the contest section, pests can be strategically stockpiled before a contest. The Vermin Trap item (craftable from Garden materials) holds up to 12 extra pests, letting you save them for the optimal contest moment. If you're serious about contest performance, you should always have Vermin Traps placed and filled before a contest crop you're strong in.

Garden Desk

The Garden Desk is a block in your Garden that tracks detailed statistics: crops farmed per hour for each crop, total contest history, plot levels, and session summaries. Use it regularly to benchmark your actual coins/hr and compare against your expected FF-based output. If your real output is significantly lower than your theoretical output, you're likely losing time to inventory management, pest disruption, or suboptimal movement patterns. The Desk also shows your Crop Milestone progress per plot, which helps you track which plots need the most farming time to level up.

Tool Progression — Hoes

Your farming hoe is the center of your Farming Fortune setup. The enchants on your hoe — Harvesting, Cultivating, Turbo-Crop, Dedication, Replenish — collectively account for hundreds of Farming Fortune at max investment. Choosing the right base hoe and reforging it correctly matters almost as much as the enchants.

The Rookie Hoe

Jacob gives you a Rookie Hoe when you complete your first Jacob's Contest (or speak to him early in farming progression). It's a functional tool with minimal base stats. Use it while you work toward Theoretical Hoes, but don't invest enchantments into it — the Theoretical Hoe series is strictly better and you'll be replacing the Rookie Hoe quickly.

Theoretical Hoe Series

There is a crop-specific Theoretical Hoe for each main crop: Theoretical Hoe of Wheat, Carrot, Potato, Sugar Cane, Cocoa, Pumpkin/Melon (shared), Mushroom, Nether Wart, and Cactus. Each Theoretical Hoe has a base stat that scales with your farming XP in that specific crop — the more XP you've accumulated farming that crop, the higher the hoe's base Farming Fortune. This scaling means the Theoretical Hoe of Sugar Cane in the hands of a veteran Sugar Cane farmer has dramatically better base stats than the same hoe held by a player who just started farming Cane.

Craft the Theoretical Hoe for your primary crop as soon as you have the materials. The crafting recipe requires crop-specific Enchanted items and some other farming materials. Once crafted, it immediately becomes your main tool and every enchant investment you make goes into it from that point forward.

Key Hoe Enchantments

The enchants that matter most, in rough priority order:

  • Turbo-[Crop] V: +25 FF for that specific crop. Buy from Anita with Jacob's Tickets. Non-negotiable priority for your main crop.
  • Cultivating X: Up to +200 FF but requires breaking 100 million crops with this specific hoe. Starts at I and levels slowly. Apply Cultivating as soon as possible so the counter starts running — even Cultivating I gives +10 FF and it levels passively as you farm.
  • Harvesting V: +50 FF universally. An enchant book that can be applied immediately. Strong value per purchase.
  • Dedication IV: Collection-scaling FF. Buy from Anita. Better the higher your crop collection is.
  • Replenish I: Auto-replants crops after harvest. Eliminates the need to manually replant Wheat, Carrot, Potato, and other replantable crops. Quality of life, not directly a FF boost, but essential for continuous farming loops.

Reforging Your Hoe — Bountiful

The Bountiful reforge is the correct reforge for every farming hoe. It gives +3 Farming Fortune per reforge level, and hoes can be reforged to high levels using the appropriate reforge stones. At maximum reforge level, Bountiful gives a substantial flat FF bonus that no other reforge comes close to matching for farming purposes. If your hoe is not Bountiful, reforge it immediately. There is no situation where a different hoe reforge beats Bountiful for pure farming output.

Melon Armor

Crafted from Enchanted Melon (160 Melon each = 1 Enchanted Melon, need multiple per armor piece), Melon Armor is your first real farming set. It gives a flat Farming Fortune set bonus when all four pieces are worn. The raw FF is modest compared to later sets, but it's the necessary starting point for the whole progression chain. Farm Melons, craft the armor, and equip the full set as soon as possible. Beyond its own stats, wearing Melon Armor causes Cropie plants to drop randomly while you farm certain crops — this is how you collect the components for the next tier.

Cropie, Squash, and Fermento Armor

These three sets each cover a subset of crops and provide Farming Fortune specifically for those crops. Cropie gives FF for Wheat, Carrot, and Potato. Squash gives FF for Melon, Pumpkin, and Cactus. Fermento gives FF for Sugar Cane, Cocoa, Nether Wart, and Mushroom. The drops that build each set only fall while you're wearing the previous tier's armor, so you need to actively wear Melon Armor while farming to get Cropie/Squash drops, and wear Cropie or Squash to get Fermento drops. This gating is intentional — it ensures players are actually farming during their gear progression rather than buying their way through.

All three sets can be worn simultaneously (one piece each is not necessary — you wear the full set of one), but in practice you wear the set that gives Farming Fortune for your current primary crop. If you're farming Pumpkin heavily, wear Squash Armor. If you're farming Cane, wear Fermento. Switching sets when switching crops is part of active farming.

Helianthus Armor — Endgame

Helianthus requires Farming 50 to wear and combines the full set bonuses of all four lower tiers into a single armor suit, then adds a large multiplier on top. Wearing Helianthus effectively gives you Melon + Cropie + Squash + Fermento bonuses simultaneously, covering all crops universally without set-switching. The crafting requires components from all four lower sets plus three special drops: Sunflower, Moonflower, and Wild Rose. These special drops only fall while wearing Helianthus prerequisites — Sunflower from farming Sunflowers while wearing Fermento, Moonflower and Wild Rose from farming Sunflowers and other crops in specific conditions. The drop rates are low, making these materials a substantial grind.

Once equipped, Helianthus dramatically simplifies your setup. No more set-swapping between crops. No more worrying about which armor to wear for a contest. Helianthus is objectively best in slot for all farming scenarios and remains so through Infernal-rarity upgrades. Enchant all four pieces with farming-relevant combat enchants for the defense values, and with Protection V for survivability against pests or other environmental damage.

Helianthus grind reality: Getting to Farming 50 takes roughly 3–6 months of consistent active farming for a typical player. The Sunflower/Moonflower/Wild Rose drops have very low rates and can take additional weeks of dedicated effort. Plan for this timeline and don't rush — every Gold medal you earn toward the cap raise is permanent progress.

Pet Guide

Pets are one of the largest single-source Farming Fortune boosts in the game. The right pet can add 50–100 FF on its own, plus often a unique mechanic that multiplies your output further. Pet choice depends on what crop you're farming and whether you're actively playing or going semi-AFK.

Legendary Elephant — General Best in Slot

The Legendary Elephant is the go-to pet for virtually all active farming situations. Its primary ability gives a large flat Farming Fortune bonus that scales with pet level, reaching approximately 100 FF at level 100. Its secondary perk causes crops broken via your Fortune to occasionally drop extra items beyond the Fortune multiplier, effectively adding another small multiplier on top of FF. The Elephant is the correct choice for any contest you're taking seriously and for any active farming session regardless of crop type.

Obtaining a Legendary Elephant requires either farming for it directly (it drops from certain crop-related activities with a low rate) or buying it from other players on the Auction House. The Legendary rarity is significantly more expensive than Epic — budget accordingly and use an Epic Elephant as a stand-in until you can afford the upgrade.

Legendary Slug — Sugar Cane Specialist

The Legendary Slug is a crop-specific pet that gives a flat bonus to Sugar Cane drops per break. Its passive ability stacks multiplicatively with Farming Fortune, not additively, making it substantially more powerful on Sugar Cane than the Elephant's additive FF contribution for that specific crop. If your primary farming crop is Sugar Cane and you're running an optimized hypermax setup, the Slug pet output on cane can slightly exceed the Elephant's contribution in raw cane-per-hour terms. For Sugar Cane contests the Slug is generally preferred over the Elephant once you have it at Legendary.

Legendary Mooshroom Cow — Mushroom Specialist

For Mushroom farming specifically, the Legendary Mooshroom Cow is best in slot. Every mushroom you break has a chance to spawn additional mushrooms nearby, which themselves can be harvested. On a well-set-up mushroom farm this creates a cascade effect that multiplies your mushroom output beyond what raw FF alone would achieve. The Mooshroom Cow is also useful for AFK Mushroom farming since the spawned mushrooms add to the spread naturally. Outside of Mushroom content, the Mooshroom Cow contributes little — swap to Elephant for everything else.

Legendary Bee — Semi-Active Harvesting

The Legendary Bee has a mechanic called Pollination: every 30 crops you break, nearby crops auto-harvest and their drops go directly to your inventory. On a dense farm layout this means a fraction of your farm is effectively harvested for free as a byproduct of harvesting the rest. The Bee is particularly good on dense row crops (Wheat, Carrot, Potato) where many plants are within auto-harvest range. It's not competitive with Elephant or Slug for pure FF or specialized drops, but it's a reasonable choice for beginner setups before you have those pets.

Rabbit — Early Leveling

The Rabbit pet gives a Farming XP boost. This is genuinely useful while you're trying to reach Farming 40 or 50, as those early XP multipliers meaningfully reduce the time to level. However, once you hit your target level and are farming for income or contest performance, Rabbit should be replaced with Elephant immediately. Holding onto a Rabbit after its XP-boosting purpose is served is one of the common beginner mistakes.

Pet Items

Equip pet items that give Farming Fortune or Farming XP depending on your current goal. The Farming Talisman pet item gives a minor FF bonus. The Book of Stats gives small stat increases across the board. For leveling sessions, use a Hyper Catalyst or similar XP-boosting pet item on your Rabbit. For active money-making sessions, prioritize any pet item that gives flat FF to your Elephant or Slug.

Gear & Accessories

Rancher's Boots

Rancher's Boots are functionally mandatory for Sugar Cane farming and highly useful for several other crops. The boots replace your standard walk speed with a configurable speed value that you set manually. The reason this matters is that crops have a fixed break animation — swinging your hoe triggers a short cooldown before the next swing registers. If you walk too fast, you'll outpace the cooldown and end up swinging at already-broken or not-yet-grown blocks. If you walk too slowly, you leave crops un-harvested for longer than necessary.

For Sugar Cane farming specifically, the community has identified optimal Rancher's Boots speed values for specific farm layouts. A standard 2-wide row layout typically runs best at 400% walk speed, though this varies based on row length and turn frequency. Experiment with your specific layout and use the Garden Desk's crops-per-hour meter to find your personal optimum. The difference between wrong speed and optimal speed can be 20–30% of hourly output.

Rancher's Boots are crafted from Golden Boots combined with Enchanted String and several farming-specific crafting materials. They're accessible to mid-game players and should be crafted as soon as you decide to commit to Sugar Cane farming.

Accessories and Talismans

Most farming-specific accessories give Farming Fortune or Farming XP bonuses. The most impactful:

  • Farmhand: Gives Farming Fortune based on total unique Visitor trades completed. Scales as you trade more visitors over time. Obtained at a specific Visitor trade milestone. Keep trading visitors consistently and this item's value grows passively.
  • Farming Talisman: A basic Farming Fortune talisman. Craft or buy early and throw it in your accessory bag immediately.
  • Pelt upgrades via accessories: Some pest-killing milestone rewards grant accessories that give additional Farming Fortune. Kill pests consistently and check the Pest control rewards track.

Beyond crop-specific accessories, standard SkyBlock accessory bag optimization applies — fill your bag with the highest-rarity general stats accessories for magic find, speed, and other secondary benefits that apply while farming.

Armor Reforges

For farming armor (Cropie, Squash, Fermento, Helianthus), the best reforges are those that give Farming Fortune. Blessed, Robust, and Blooming are the primary candidates depending on which is available via reforge stone and which gives more FF for the specific armor tier you're reforging. Check the current stat comparisons for your tier — the optimal reforge changes between armor tiers because the base stats differ. As a general rule, prioritize any reforge that directly lists Farming Fortune, then secondary farming stats, over defensive or offensive stats which you don't need while farming.

Farming XP Methods — Beginner to Endgame

Beginner: Farming 1–20

At this stage your only goal is getting to Farming 10 for Jacob's Contests and then to Farming 20 to unlock better gear tiers. Plant Wheat, Carrot, and Potato on your private island in a simple grid. Use Replenish enchant on your starting hoe as soon as possible so you don't have to manually replant. Do not over-invest in your private island farm — you'll abandon it for the Garden soon.

When you hit Farming 10, immediately start doing every Jacob's Contest. Even with a mediocre setup and only Bronze medals, you earn tickets. Those early tickets should go toward Turbo-Wheat or Turbo-Cane (whichever crop you're focusing on). Bronze medals also count toward Visitor trade unlocks and give you the collection count that powers Dedication later. No contest participation is wasted.

Unlock the Garden as soon as you hit 100 crop collection — this happens naturally, usually around Farming 8–12. The Garden opens immediately and you can start setting up your first plots right away. Even a level 1 Garden plot with a basic farm is better than your private island for both XP and income.

Midgame: Farming 20–40

This is where farming starts becoming a real system. Your priorities: build out your Garden plots for 2–3 crops, get Melon Armor crafted, start the armor chain toward Cropie/Squash, and push for Gold medals in your 1–2 best crops. Spend tickets on Turbo-Crop books before anything else. Get a Theoretical Hoe for your main crop and apply Harvesting V and Cultivating I immediately.

At Farming 30 you can start targeting Fermento Armor drops. The armor chain grinds (Melon → Cropie/Squash → Fermento) take several weeks of active farming even with focused effort. Don't rush it — farm your main crop for money and income, wear the appropriate armor set, and collect the next tier's drops as a natural byproduct. By Farming 40 you should have Fermento fully crafted and be working toward Farming 50 for Helianthus eligibility.

Aim for Gold medals in at least 2 crops during this stage. The cap unlock from Gold medals directly enables higher Farming XP accumulation — each cap raise lets you push further in Farming skill, which adds more base FF passively.

Late/Endgame: Farming 40–60

By Farming 40 you should be generating meaningful income. The remaining goals are long-term grinds: Helianthus armor (requires Farming 50 and Sunflower/Moonflower/Wild Rose drops), Legendary Elephant pet, Cultivating X (100 million crops with one hoe — this takes several months of daily farming), all Turbo-Crop V on your main crops, Bountiful reforge on the hoe at maximum reforge level, all relevant Garden plots at level 12.

Farming 60 requires Gold medals in every crop to fully raise the cap. Even players who've been active for over a year sometimes lack Gold in a few niche crops (Cocoa, Nether Wart, Mushroom) because they're unpopular contest crops. Every time Jacob announces one of your missing Gold crops, prioritize that contest regardless of your other gear — a single Gold is a permanent cap unlock.

Hypermax Farming Fortune varies depending on exact setup but realistic targets are 800–1000 FF for a player with Helianthus, Legendary Elephant, Bountiful hoe, Cultivating X, all Turbo-Crop V, and max plot levels. The theoretical ceiling with perfect investment in every source is higher but reflects the gap between "very good setup" and "no-life maximum".

AFK vs Active Farming

The question of whether to AFK farm or active farm is largely answered by which crop you're farming. Most crops in SkyBlock cannot be meaningfully AFK farmed — they require a player to physically break blocks, and the difference in output between an active player and an idle player is roughly the difference between making money and making nothing. There is one important exception.

AFK Mushroom Farming

Brown and Red Mushrooms spread naturally on Mycelium and Dirt blocks under specific lighting conditions. If you build a Mushroom farm in your Garden with Mycelium flooring and appropriate spacing, mushrooms will spread from your initial placement and gradually cover available squares. You can then walk through periodically to collect the spread mushrooms, or set up a more elaborate collection system. The coins-per-hour ceiling is low (100–300k/hr at best, even with max Mooshroom Cow setup) compared to active Pumpkin or Cane farming, but the attention requirement is near zero. Some players run Mushroom AFK while doing something else on a second monitor, treating the coins as essentially free background income.

Mushroom AFK farming is entirely legal and within Hypixel's rules. The caveats: Mushroom prices fluctuate on the Bazaar, your Mooshroom Cow pet level significantly affects output, and the farm needs occasional attention to harvest accumulated mushrooms before plots fill up. Set a timer and check every 30–60 minutes when running AFK sessions.

Active Farming — Cane and Pumpkin

For Sugar Cane, the correct technique is to set Rancher's Boots to optimal speed, hold W, and strafe left and right at the end of each cane row to turn around without breaking momentum. The goal is a continuous motion with no pauses — every second you're not breaking blocks is income lost. Keep your hotbar organized so inventory management can be done without stopping movement.

For Pumpkin, sprint through your harvest lane at full speed clicking continuously. Pumpkin blocks break quickly and the stem immediately re-queues, so dense plots with short harvest lanes (frequent turns) actually produce more pumpkins per hour than long straight lanes because you spend more time over populated stems and less time in transit. Design your Pumpkin plot with short lanes and maximum stem density per harvest lane.

For Melon the technique is identical to Pumpkin. The main difference is that Melon blocks sometimes require a second click due to their size — test your setup and adjust if you notice blocks being missed.

Semi-AFK Methods

Some players use keyboard configurations or macro setups. It is important to understand that Hypixel's rules prohibit automated gameplay macros, auto-clickers, and bots. Using these is a permanent ban risk. There are, however, legal keyboard remapping setups that require continuous physical input but reduce hand strain — these don't automate the game, they just make the input more ergonomic. The distinction matters. Do not use any setup that removes the requirement for continuous player input.

Why Pumpkin Is Typically #1

Pumpkin consistently leads the coins/hr leaderboard for several reasons that reinforce each other. First, Pumpkin Bazaar prices are historically stable — demand comes from multiple sources including Minion fuel and building materials, keeping the floor price resilient. Second, the farming mechanic allows dense plot layouts with minimal wasted space — you can fit a lot of active stems per square meter of Garden plot compared to row crops. Third, Squash Armor (which you wear on the path to Helianthus) provides bonus Farming Fortune specifically for Pumpkin, giving Pumpkin farmers a bonus FF window during the mid-game armor grind. Fourth, Pumpkin is one of the most popular Jacob's Contest crops among high-level farmers, meaning optimizing for Pumpkin Diamond medals and optimizing for Pumpkin income are the same optimization.

Sugar Cane's Competitiveness

Sugar Cane's gap relative to Pumpkin at hypermax is mostly explained by the Slug pet versus Elephant pet comparison — at true hypermax (Legendary Slug), Cane can approach Pumpkin's ceiling. The Slug's multiplicative cane drop bonus stacks on top of Farming Fortune in a way that significantly boosts cane output beyond what additive FF alone would suggest. Players who invest in a Legendary Slug specifically for Cane farming sometimes report cane income slightly exceeding Pumpkin on their specific setups. In practice, both crops are in the same tier and the right choice depends on which you're more set up for.

Bazaar Price Monitoring

The raw coins/hr numbers above assume average prices. Bazaar prices for farming crops shift based on SkyBlock update cycles, Mayor elections, and natural supply-demand fluctuations. Always check current prices before a long farming session. If Pumpkin prices spike 30% above average due to a crafting update, your Pumpkin income spikes 30% too. Conversely if someone floods the Bazaar with Sugar Cane, your Cane income drops proportionally. Flexible farmers who can swap between Pumpkin and Cane within the same Garden setup can chase whichever has the better Bazaar price at any given time.

META Strategies

Contest META

For maximum contest medal performance the approach is specialization over breadth. Pick one or two crops — typically Pumpkin and Sugar Cane — and maximize your setup for those specifically. Turbo-Crop V for both, full Garden plots for both, Squash/Fermento armor for whichever crop is up, Bountiful Theoretical Hoe per crop, Legendary Elephant for general contests or crop-specific pet when appropriate. Pre-spawn pests on the relevant crop's plots using Vermin Traps. Kill all 20 pests at contest start. Aim for Diamond in your best crop and Platinum in your second. Don't spread time farming crops you're weak in — a Silver in your third-best crop earns fewer tickets than a Platinum in your strongest crop and takes the same time.

When Finnegan is elected (GOATed), the percentage cutoffs relax significantly. This is the time to push for Diamond and Platinum in crops you'd normally only reach Gold or Platinum in. Finnegan election windows are also when slower players can catch up on Gold medal cap unlocks — even players with mediocre setups can hit Gold tier in most crops during GOATed since the cutoff drops to top 20%.

Income META

For pure coin-per-hour the income META is: Pumpkin or Sugar Cane as primary crop, Legendary Elephant (or Slug for Cane), full Helianthus at Infernal rarity if accessible, Bountiful Theoretical Hoe with Cultivating X + Harvesting V + Turbo-Crop V + Dedication IV, all relevant Garden plots at level 12, Farmhand accessory at high visitor count, all Pelt upgrades purchased, Farming Fortune at 800+ total. This setup requires months of sustained effort but once assembled it generates income competitive with most mid-to-late endgame combat money-making methods with significantly less execution requirement.

Infernal Helianthus (upgrading the rarity from Legendary to Infernal using Infernal Crimson Essence) is the max-investment endgame armor upgrade. The FF bonus increase from Legendary to Infernal is significant — several hundred additional FF — but the cost in Essence is very high. Treat Infernal Helianthus as a long-term goal, not an immediate purchase. Legendary Helianthus is already the best setup most players will ever use.

Hybrid Approach

The most time-efficient overall strategy for profile-wide progression combines active farming during contest windows with passive mushroom or minion income between contests. During the 20-minute Jacob's Contest window, go absolutely maximum effort — Elephant pet, best crop, full Fortune setup, pest burst at start. Outside of contests, either farm actively for sustained income if you want to maximize coins/hr, or switch to AFK Mushroom and let your farming minions (if you have them) run on your private island. The contest window every real hour is an anchor point you should organize your play session around: every hour, 20 minutes of max-effort farming. What you do with the remaining 40 minutes is up to your goals.

Common Mistakes

Farming has a lot of moving parts and most new farmers make the same errors. Here are the most impactful mistakes and why they're costly:

Not getting Farming 10 early. Jacob's Contests start at Farming 10 and run every hour. Every hour you don't have Farming 10 is a contest missed — and those early Bronze medals, even with terrible gear, build toward visitor milestones and ticket accumulation. A player who gets Farming 10 in week one and does every contest will have significantly more Turbo-Crop enchant levels than a player who hits Farming 10 in week three, even if both do contests equally after that point.

Ignoring the Garden after unlocking it. The Garden unlocks easily and many players pop into it, look around, and go back to their private island farms. This is a mistake. Plot levels, Visitor trades, Farmhand progression, Composter income, and pest management all require active engagement with the Garden system. The longer you wait to set up your Garden plots and start leveling them, the further behind you fall on the FF bonuses those levels provide.

Using the wrong pet. The Rabbit pet giving Farming XP is useful only while actively leveling. Once you hit your target Farming level milestone, switch to Elephant or your crop-specific pet immediately. Holding onto Rabbit for sentimental reasons costs real coins per hour. Similarly, using a low-level Elephant (Common or Uncommon rarity) is significantly weaker than a Legendary — upgrade or save up for the correct rarity before committing.

Not reforging the hoe to Bountiful. An unreforged hoe or a hoe reforged to something non-Bountiful is leaving free Farming Fortune on the table. This is a simple fix: go to the Reforge Anvil, apply the Bountiful reforge stone (or use the standard reforge if Bountiful reforge stone applies) to your main hoe. It's one of the highest FF gains per coin spent in the whole game.

Buying Turbo-Crop IV and V before earning the prerequisite medals. Turbo-Crop levels 4 and 5 for any given crop require you to have earned at least a Bronze or Silver medal in that specific crop. If you spend tickets expecting to apply the book and find it's locked, those tickets are wasted until you earn the prerequisite. Always check which medal you need for the next Turbo-Crop level and earn it before purchasing.

Spreading Farming Fortune equally across all crops instead of specializing. Farming Fortune from Turbo-Crop enchants is crop-specific. A player with Turbo-Cane V and no Turbo-Pumpkin will have dramatically better Sugar Cane output than Pumpkin output. Spreading your ticket spending equally across all nine crops means you have mediocre Fortune everywhere and excellent Fortune nowhere. Pick 1–2 crops, max their Turbo-Crop books first, then consider spreading to secondary crops.

Forgetting to kill pests between contests. Alive pests reduce plot farming efficiency and block Vermin Trap capacity. If you never kill pests, your plots are always running below optimal, your Vermin Traps are always full and blocking new pest spawns, and you miss out on Pelt currency and pest accessory materials. Build a habit of clearing pests at the end of every farming session. It takes 2–3 minutes and meaningfully affects your long-term Farming Fortune via Pelt upgrades.

Not monitoring Bazaar prices. The temptation to always sell raw crops is strong, but sometimes processing or holding is better. Conversely, some players process crops into Enchanted versions to sell for more, not realizing that the Bazaar price of the Enchanted version doesn't always justify the crafting overhead. Check both the raw crop price and the Enchanted version price every session. At some volumes, selling raw is better; at others, crafting first is better. The math changes with market conditions.

Macro warning: Automated farming macros are against Hypixel's rules and result in permanent bans. The appeal is obvious given the repetitive nature of active farming, but no amount of coins is worth a permaban on an account with months or years of progression. If your hands hurt from farming, take breaks or invest more in AFK Mushroom setups. Do not use macros.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I raise my Farming cap past 50?

The Farming skill cap starts at 50. Each crop has an associated cap raise of +1, unlocked by earning a Gold medal in that crop's Jacob's Farming Contest. There are 9 primary crops and potentially one additional raise, summing to a hard cap of Farming 60. You don't need Gold in all crops to progress — each Gold you earn unlocks +1 to the cap regardless of order. Work on the crops you're strongest in first.

What's the best crop for a beginner?

Wheat, Carrot, or Potato for the absolute beginning — they require no special tools, grow on basic farmland, and auto-replant with Replenish. Once you have Rancher's Boots crafted (mid-game), Sugar Cane becomes highly rewarding. Once you have Squash Armor, Pumpkin is the target for maximum income. Don't try to start with Pumpkin or Cane immediately — the gear dependencies make early Pumpkin/Cane farming significantly weaker than it should be at proper gear levels.

What does Helianthus Armor actually require to craft?

Farming 50 to wear (you can craft it at any level but can't equip it). Crafting requires components from all four preceding farming armor sets (Melon, Cropie, Squash, Fermento), plus the three special flower drops: Sunflower, Moonflower, and Wild Rose. Sunflower drops from farming Sunflowers while wearing Fermento Armor. Moonflower and Wild Rose have lower drop rates and specific farming conditions. The full Helianthus craft is a substantial material investment on top of the month-long Fermento armor grind that precedes it.

Is AFK farming worth it?

Only for Mushrooms. Every other crop produces negligible income without active player input because crop growth rates are slow enough that you'd earn almost nothing sitting still. Mushroom is the one exception because Mushrooms spread autonomously on Mycelium. The income ceiling is much lower than active Pumpkin or Cane farming, but if you want truly passive income while doing other things, Mushroom AFK is the only legitimate option. Do not attempt to AFK other crops — it doesn't work and you'll just come back to an empty inventory.

What's the point of the Composter?

The Composter converts excess crops into Organic Matter, which is used in various Garden upgrades and some crafting recipes. It also provides a passive sink for low-value crops (excess Wheat, Carrot, Potato) that aren't worth selling on the Bazaar at current prices. Upgrading the Composter speed and capacity is a good use of early Organic Matter, since a faster Composter produces more Organic Matter per day from the same crop input.

Why do my crops/hr numbers seem lower than what I see posted online?

Four main reasons: First, the posted numbers are often from hypermax setups with 800+ Farming Fortune — early and mid-game setups have much lower output. Second, movement efficiency matters enormously: pausing, inventory management, and suboptimal routing all reduce your effective breaks-per-minute. Third, Bazaar prices fluctuate — the coins/hr estimates assume average prices. Fourth, Garden plot level matters — a level 1 plot gives much less FF than a level 12 plot, directly reducing output. Check the Garden Desk for your actual per-crop output and compare it against your theoretical FF-based maximum to identify your specific bottleneck.

Do I need all 23 Garden plots active?

No. You unlock plots progressively and don't need all 23. Most dedicated farmers use 3–5 plots for their primary crops and 1 plot each for secondary crops they need for Visitor trades. Having plots assigned and leveling passively is better than leaving them unassigned, but don't feel pressured to actively farm all 23 plots simultaneously — specializing 5–8 plots at high levels outperforms spreading effort across all 23 at low levels every time.

Is there any reason to farm Sunflowers?

Only for Helianthus crafting components. Sunflower, Moonflower, and Wild Rose are required for the Helianthus recipe and only drop while wearing specific farming armor tiers. Once you have enough for your Helianthus craft, there's no reason to continue farming Sunflowers. They don't have significant Bazaar value and aren't a contest crop.

How important is the Farmhand item really?

More important than most players realize. Farmhand scales with cumulative unique Visitor trades, and at high visitor counts it gives 30+ Farming Fortune from a single accessory slot. The compounding nature means players who trade every visitor from their first day have significantly more Farming Fortune from Farmhand alone than players who start trading seriously at Farming 40. The lesson: trade every visitor you see, even Copper tier, from the moment you unlock the Garden.
