Dungeons Guide

The Catacombs — floor requirements, classes, gear recommendations, scoring, and Master Mode.

v0.21

Dungeons are team PvE content where 1–5 players work through procedurally generated rooms before fighting a boss. They're unlocked through Combat 15 and are the main driver of gear progression from midgame onward.

Getting Started

Talk to Mort in the Hub (Graveyard area) to unlock The Catacombs. You need Combat 15 minimum. To enter solo from Floor III onward you need Saul's Recommendation, which requires completing F3 in a team first.

Each floor requires a specific Catacombs level — this goes up with every floor you complete. You gain Catacombs XP from run completions, boss kills, and daily objectives.

Classes

Pick one class per run. Your class choice affects what gear scales and what abilities you get. You can swap between runs.

ClassRoleBest for
HealerKeeps team alive with healing orbs and aurasSupport, high-level group runs
BerserkMelee damage, Life Steal sustainFast clears, most popular DPS class
MageMagic damage, requires high IntelligenceF5–F7, Hyperion users
ArcherRanged DPS, kills high-HP targets safelyBowmen, Terminator users in Master Mode
TankAbsorbs damage, draws aggro from teamSurvivability-first runs, M7

Each class has a Leveling Bonus that scales stats as you gain class XP. Running one class consistently is significantly better than spreading XP across all five.

Dungeon Mechanics

A few mechanics that catch new players off guard:

  • Speed cap: Any speed above 100 is reduced by 33%. Dragon armor's speed bonus works differently inside dungeons.
  • Potions: You can only consume one potion per run (except Healing, Stamina, and Mana potions).
  • Death: You become a ghost and can still interact with the run. Coins in your purse are safe — dungeons don't take your coins on death.
  • Wither Keys: Each floor drops two Wither Keys and one Blood Key from killing all starred mobs in specific rooms. Blood Key opens the Blood Room.
  • Essence: Dropped from bosses and rooms — spend at Ophelia or other NPCs to upgrade Dungeon items.

Scoring & Grades

Your run score is made up of four categories: Skill (deaths + puzzle fails), Exploration (rooms + secrets), Speed (completion time), and Bonus (extra). Aim for 300+ for S+.

S+ grants a Plushie drop chance and better chest contents. For high scores, secrets are the biggest factor — explore every room and open all secret locations.

Gear by Stage

F1–F3

Zombie Knight Armor or Hardened Diamond gets you through F1–F2. Aim for Strong Dragon or Unstable Dragon before F3. Aspect of the End or AotD works fine as a weapon here.

F4–F5

F4 is where Bonzo's Mask stops trivializing deaths — you need real defense. Shadow Assassin Chestplate from F5 is a major upgrade. Livid Dagger from F5 should be your next weapon goal — get it Fabled and Recombobulated.

F6–F7

Shadow Assassin full set or Storm's Armor for Mage. F7 needs ~700 Defense and Catacombs 28+. You're working toward Necron's Handle here. Hyperion requires the Handle plus crafting materials — it's the main reason people grind F7.

Master Mode

Master Mode (M1–M7) is a harder version unlocked after completing all 7 floors. Mobs have dramatically higher HP and damage. Each Master floor has its own Catacombs level requirement on top of the normal floor requirement.

FloorCata Level (rough)Notes
M1~27Good starting point for Master Mode
M3~31–32Defense becomes critical
M4~36MP 1000+ strongly recommended
M5~38–40Terminator required — Juju won't work here
M6~41–44Master Stars on armor equal to floor number
M745+Best-in-slot: Divan's Head, Skeleton Master Chest, Necron Legs, Maxor Boots

Master Stars are applied to Dungeon gear using Star Fragments from Master Mode chests. Add gemstones and wax your equipment before starring — order matters.
